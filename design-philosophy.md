# Darel Veterinary Clinic — Design Philosophy

## Movement: **"Calm Practice"**

A modern premium veterinary aesthetic for a thirty-year clinic that has, to date, been digitally invisible. The visual references are not the academic museum publication, not the cold clinical hospital, and certainly not the cheerful pastel-paw chain template — they are **Modern Animal** in Los Angeles, **Small Door Veterinary** in New York, and **Bond Vet** in Brooklyn. These are the brands that have proven what a premium independent veterinary practice looks like online in the 2020s: warm, calm, contemporary, trustworthy, and **human-scaled**. Darel has thirty years of substance and zero of the visual presence those brands earned in five.

Form is **modern editorial-medical**. The site is laid out to answer the questions a premium pet owner actually has — *who is the head veterinarian, what equipment is on site, what does this cost, and can I bring my dog on a Saturday* — with the same calm professionalism a high-end private medical practice extends to its patients. Sticky-scroll for the chief vet's biography, an open price list under each service, an equipment register with manufacturer and year, and an honest hours panel that fixes the weekday-only constraint with new weekend reception. Specifics replace adjectives, prices replace silence, and the chief vet's signature replaces stock photography.

Colour is **soft warm trust** and nothing else. **Off-white paper** (#faf8f3) is the page — warm, not the cold white of a hospital, not the stark white of a software product. **Soft warm charcoal** (#2a2826), with a touch of brown undertone, is the primary text — confident but never cold. **Deep sage** (#5a7861) is the brand colour — a calm, trustworthy green that signals care without the clinical-blue cliché of every chain veterinary site, and is distinct from every other green already used in this portfolio. **Muted sage** (#8fa194) appears as decorative surface and large display element. **Champagne brass** (#c4a772) is the single premium accent — used the way a high-end private medical practice uses brass on a door plate, once a section, where the eye should land on a price or the chief vet's name. **Soft blush** (#e8c8b4) is the rare warmth highlight — not pink, not pastel, just the quiet warmth of a waiting room sofa.

Typography is **modern editorial × friendly geometric**, three voices that pair without competing. **Instrument Serif** is the display voice — a contemporary editorial serif with an unusually warm italic, used by the design-led publications and product brands that defined the 2020s premium aesthetic. **Figtree** is the body voice — a friendly, geometric humanist sans designed for screen reading at small sizes; warm without being cute, professional without being corporate. **Geist Mono** is reserved for prices, hours, equipment models and phone numbers — the technical truths of a clinic, set in tabular figures the way a clear estimate should be set. None of these three has been used in the previous seven commissions in this portfolio.

Geometry is **soft**. Where the previous direction used 2-4 pixel corner radii out of editorial discipline, **Calm Practice** uses 8-16 pixel radii throughout — the visual softness that distinguishes a premium private clinic from a hospital ward. Cards have generous padding, sections breathe, the hero ornament is a **soft pulse of breath rings around a single quiet heart** — not a crosshair, not an anatomical compass. The page is welcoming where it can be and rigorous where it must be.

Motion is governed by ui-ux-pro-max rules: **150–300 ms** on micro-interactions, **transform/opacity only** (per `transform-performance`), **motion-meaning** (every animation expresses cause-effect), **reduced-motion** fully honoured. The Motion library carries: a hero with character-by-character entrance under `spring-physics`; a calm marquee of services running across one band like the line of a printed referral list; a sticky-scroll chief-veterinarian biography; **three-dimensional tilt** on service cards that mimics a card lifted softly from a counter; a **magnetic CTA** at the booking moment; a counter that fills as the eye reaches the thirty-year stat; line-by-line manifesto reveal timed to a slow reading pace, not a marketing-attention pace.

Composition is asymmetric, ruled, generous. Where another vet site would print *"We love your pets!"* with a stock photo of a smiling dog, this one prints *"Thirty years of practice. Two operating theatres. One head veterinarian since 1995."* in soft Instrument Serif italic on warm off-white and lets the specifics replace the sentiment. The page is what a thirty-year premium veterinary clinic should have always had: a calm, modern, professional brand presence that looks as carefully tended as the practice itself.
