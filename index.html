— I The chief veterinarian

A practice built on one signature.

Darel has been led by the same head veterinarian since the practice opened in 1995. Three decades of single-handed continuity is unusual in any medical field; in private veterinary work in Central Asia, it is rare.

Darel opened in 1995 — when there was no premium veterinary practice in Tashkent for cats and dogs at all, only mixed-animal state services and a handful of part-time small-animal surgeons. Our chief vet was one of those surgeons.

In thirty years he has performed several thousand soft-tissue and orthopaedic operations, taught a generation of younger veterinarians, and held — uniquely for a private clinic of this age in Tashkent — the same surgical signature on the discharge note from year one to year thirty.

Continuity is the entire premium. A six-year-old cat we operated in 2020 comes back in 2024 for a follow-up ultrasound, and the same person who performed the surgery reads the scan. No chain hospital can sell that.

We are not the cheapest clinic in Tashkent — we are openly recognised as one of the most expensive — and we do not apologise for that. The price reflects three decades of one signature, two operating theatres, and equipment we replace before it ages out, not after.

If you are reading this at midnight because your cat has stopped eating, book the next morning, by name, with the chief vet.
